Re-entry Services

What we do

Through community and government partnerships, The Last Mile aims to help turn the technology and business skills learned by the justice-involved person into careers. The Last Mile Workforce Reentry Program accelerates opportunities for Returned Citizens to find jobs in the technology and business sectors.

Step 1: Joining The Program

Once an offender joins the program we can start teaching them valuable coding and life skills for when they get out

Step 2: Education

The Offender uses his time to learn as much code as possible for when she gets out,

Step 3: Re-Entry

The Offender reenters society with a knowledgy of coding and life skills and can use our reentry services and connections to find him or herself a job.

What we

teach in our curriculums

Students first learn graphic design and UI/UX elements before learning HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap within the first track. Students then start to learn Javascript and Jquery later in the first track before having to do a final project displaying all their new found skills.

If students decide to continue in the program into track 2 they can learn the server side back end coding for web design. We call this the M.E.R.N track where we teach MongoDb, Express, ReactJS, and NodeJS.

Students can continue as in our free venture program or learn more.

Education

The program provides computer coding training to prepare its students for release into today’s high- tech environment. Currently, there are nearly 1 million unfilled software engineering jobs, which means TLM graduates could be well-positioned to qualify for many of these jobs. TLM coding classrooms are in California, Indiana, Kansas, Oklahoma and Michigan, and are expanding to other states.

+

Reputation

The Last Mile (TLM) is a nonprofit founded in 2010 at San Quentin to address the societal impact of incarceration as a drain on human and fiscal resources. TLM operates the first full stack coding program inside US prisons. The program's mission is to provide marketable skills that result in gainful employment. The core belief is that having a job is the key to successful reentry and breaking the cycle of incarceration.

Our benefits

Today there are approximately 2.5 million people incarcerated in the United States. Most of these people (95%) will return home to their families and communities on parole (Bureau of Justice Statistics). The unemployment rate for these Returning Citizens is about five times higher than that of the general population. The lack of meaningful employment opportunities for Returned Citizens is a primary factor driving America’s 64% recidivism rate. A lack of adequate job preparation during incarceration greatly increases the likelihood of people reoffending and returning to prison upon their release. Meanwhile, the technology industry has an estimated 1 million US engineering jobs unfulfilled because there are not enough trained individuals to fill these positions.

Reentry

The Last Mile’s Reentry Department supports the successful reintegration of Returned Citizens back into society, the workforce, and their families. We accomplish this by providing business and technology resources, as well as opportunities through our network of Fair Chance employers.

Academic support

Our participants receive 18 months of intense technical training which prepares them to enter the workforce as software engineers. In addition to the technical training our participants receive, The Last Mile understands that to be successful, our participants must have the soft skills, emotional intelligence and self-awareness to ensure a fulfilling career.

Our Reentry Team

To better assist our participants in their next journey, we conduct a Reentry Assessment which helps us support them in creating a tailored pathway for success. The Reentry Assessment is an evaluation of the participants’ desires, skills and passions. This process includes a technical component which evaluates the participants' projects and technical abilities. This helps us to create learning pathways for those who would like to continue their education.

Future Of TLM

As we grow, The Last Mile continues to form alliances with strategic partners who can help us in our mission. We have created alliances with coding bootcamps, such as Hack Reactor and General Assembly, which allow our graduates to continue their education. We have a strong relationship with Next Chapter, a program at Slack designed to shift the perception about the potential of justice-involved people and to generate new opportunities in skilled long-term employment in the technology sector for Returned Citizens.
